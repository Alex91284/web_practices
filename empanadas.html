Classic Empanadas Recipe

Look no further for a classic recipe for stew empanadas. Accompanied by a delicious vegetable aji, no one will be able to deny its simple decadence. For best results, fry in very hot oil.

Instructions

Compound or Stew (Empanada Stuffing)

  1. Chop the potato, previously washed and peeled. Put the potato in a deep pot.
  2. On top of the potato add the ground beef. Add the long onion, previously chopped and saut√©ed, add the spices (ricostilla and color), the salt.
  3. Finally, the water is added.
  4. Cook over high heat, when it reaches boiling point, lower the heat to medium and leave it for 30 minutes more.
  5. After this time, the potato is tested to see if it is soft, then stir with a large (wooden) spoon to incorporate all the ingredients and let it rest for 45 minutes.

Cornmeal Dough

  1. Bring the water to a boil.
  2. Pour the cornmeal into a wide-mouthed container. Add the remaining ingredients (salt and sugar).
  3. Hot water is added, stir with a spoon and let it stand for 25 minutes.
  4. Then knead with your hands for 15 minutes, until the dough comes off from the palm of your hand.
  5. Portion it into medium or small balls depending on the size you want and the quantity you want to fry.
  6. With a bag and a flat-bottomed plate, make the plates and fill them with stew with a spoon, close the plate forming the empanada.
  7. In a bowl with the oil previously heated, dip the empanadas and leave them until they are golden brown.

Vegetables Chili

  1. In a wide-mouthed bowl, grate the onion and tomato and chop the cilantro.
  2. In a chopping board, add salt and grind the chili bell pepper with a stone and incorporate it with the other ingredients.
  3. Finally add the oil and stir.

Serve and enjoy a delicious snack.
